The other clergyman, Thomas Malthus, 1766-1834, is remembered for a theory named after him. His essay, On the Principle of Population, posited a crowded planet where food supply is outstripped by population growth, so that starvation and death result. At the time, 1798, earth’s population was about 800 million only. Now, the same number, if not more, go to bed hungry every night.
