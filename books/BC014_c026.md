Also, I shed tears of self-pity as I slept on the sidewalks to await the morning newspapers which I sold to our neighbors in Sampaloc, Manila. I wrote in my diary: "Today, I am a newsboy. But someday, I'll be president of a major newspaper." I thought that was just a pipe dream of an ambitious poor boy, but in March 1991, by the grace of God, I became president of the Philippine Daily Inquirer.
