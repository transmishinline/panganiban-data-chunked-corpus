President Corazon Cojuangco Aquino appointed all the justices when she ascended to power. Yet, she did not always get what she wanted. The Court remained generally independent. President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo, during her nine-and-a-half-year incumbency that is second in length only to Marcos' 21, named a majority and eventually the totality of the high tribunal, including three chief justices.
