As a poor newsboy, I valued each precious centavo, as I earned only 2 centavos per newspaper I sold. (Broadsheets and bottled Coke during those bygone days sold for only 10 centavos each. One US dollar exchanged for two pesos.) Perhaps amused at my untiring haggling, he offered me a grand deal: If I could bring 30 of my schoolmates to his store, he would give my chosen pair for free.
