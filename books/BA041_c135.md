Ignorance of the law on the part of a judge is not only most ignominious, it is also prejudicial to litigants and the administration of justice as a whole. Magistrates are well-advised to keep abreast of the latest in legislation and jurisprudence, and avoid dealing out injustice and reaping embarrassment for themselves. (*Valat v. Judge Ramos, *A.M. No. MTJ-91-56 7, December 6, 1996)
