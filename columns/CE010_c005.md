At seven, she formally debuted, and at 20, she won the prestigious Leventritt Gold Medal in New York—the first Filipino to do so—and has since stunned audiences with her interpretations of Chopin, Rachmaninoff, and Tchaikovsky, among others. She performed with the world’s greatest orchestras and conductors, from the Philippine Philharmonic to the New York Philharmonic to the London Philharmonic.
