If he was the major catalyst of the bloodless Edsa revolution in 1986, he remained the awesome pillar of the constitutional democracy created by that cathartic transition. Though a little frail of body, JPE is as sharp, as quick-witted, as eloquent, as strong-willed, and yes, as suave, now as when he was my dashing professor at the Far Eastern University Institute of Law some 50 years ago.
