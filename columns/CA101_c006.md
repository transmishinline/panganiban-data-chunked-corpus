Home Development Mutual Fund (June 19, 2000) held that “the legal meaning of the word ‘and/or’ should be taken in its ordinary signification, i.e., ‘either and or;’ e.g., butter and/or eggs means butter and eggs, or butter or eggs … the intention of the legislature in using the term ‘and/or’ is that the word ‘and’ and the word ‘or’ are to be used interchangeably.”
