Ladies and gentlemen, this was not the first time my life was at a real risk of termination. When I was six years old, I was stricken with dysentery in a barrio of my parent's hometown of Candaba, Pampanga during World War II. Our country was occupied by the Japanese armed forces. There was no hospital, no medicine, not even a doctor or nurse to attend to me in the small barrio where we lived.
