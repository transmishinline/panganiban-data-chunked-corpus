To impeach requires the vote of only one-third of all House members, and to convict only two-thirds vote of all the senators (16 of 24). In contrast, the 1936 Charter required two-thirds vote to impeach, and three-fourths vote (18 of 24) to convict. Moreover, "graft and corruption … and betrayal of public trust" were added as new impeachment grounds. Also, our people have become more proactive.
