The Inquirer recently reported (on 1/4/2020) that some 160,000 cases pended in the country’s 1,200 first level courts and 640,000 in the 1,100 second level courts. (The same figures were cited in my column two years ago on Jan. 13, 2018.) These stats compute to an average of 133 cases per first level court (160,000/1,200=133) and 582 cases per second level court (640,000/1,100=582).
