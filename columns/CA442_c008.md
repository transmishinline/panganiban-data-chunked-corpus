The following year, 1975, a Philippine government (GPH) panel held meetings with the MNLF in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, that led to the signing of the Tripoli Agreement in 1976 outlining the autonomy—no longer independence—aspirations of the MNLF. In 1977, a leadership rivalry divided the MNLF with the formation of the MILF led by Hashim Salamat.
