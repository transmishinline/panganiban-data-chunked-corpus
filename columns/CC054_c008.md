I was initially given 1,200 [should be 1,100] cases when I started as an associate justice in 1995, plus 30 [should be 45] more raffled to me every month thereafter. I reduced them to 200, all of them current, upon my retirement as chief justice in 2006. When I was CJ, I instituted ‘Operation Zero Backlog’ precisely to tackle delayed cases in the entire Court.
