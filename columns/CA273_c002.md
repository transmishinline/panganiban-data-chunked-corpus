As background, the automated machines counted ballots that were 25-percent shaded. However, the PET has ruled that only those shaded to the extent of 50 percent should be considered, thereby unintentionally disenfranchising those who shaded their ballots between the "25- and 50-percent gap." To avoid this disenfranchisement, my June 14 column backed a reversion to the 25-percent threshold.
