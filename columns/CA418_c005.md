Nonetheless, our Supreme Court has nullified agreed rates that are “unconscionable or excessive.” What is unconscionable or excessive is difficult to define. For example, in 2001 (Solangon vs Salazar), the Court struck down a stipulated rate of 72 percent per year and reduced it to 12 percent. But in 2002 (Silvestre vs Ramos), it upheld an agreed rate of 7 percent per month.
